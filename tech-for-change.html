Your challenge: create a prototype device to protect our wildlife

At Science Oxford, we have a large woodland, ponds and meadows.

You can use use technology to design a solution to a problem that effects the wildlife living here.

Some problems we have include:

  • Where should we plant new trees?
  • How can we protect new growth from deer?
  • How can we stop the ponds drying out?
  • Where should we put new bird or bat boxes?

Next steps

The micro:bit has many different sensors you can use. To decide where to start, think about what problem you want to solve.

Some sensors are in the inputs menu:

  • light level - the micro:bit uses the LED screen on the front of the micro:bit to measure light
  • temperature - the micro:bit uses a sensor on the processor on the back to measure temperature
  • compass heading - the micro:bit gives you an angle between 0-359 degrees. 0 is North, 180 is South, but it is hard to get it pointing exactly
  • accelerometer - use the gestures, such as shake, to tell when the micro:bit is moving in certain ways
  • sound level - the micro:bit V2 uses a microphone on the front of the micro:bit to measure sound levels

You can also use other outputs, such as a speaker and the Music blocks.
